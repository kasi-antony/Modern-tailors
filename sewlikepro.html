SEW LIKE PROFESSIONAL

  1. Press as you sew. Good pressing will make your sewing look so much better!
  2. Make sure that you cut accurately and that you transfer your markings to the cut fabric accurately.
  3. Sew as precisely as you can. Use the markings on your sewing machine to help you sew with an
    accurate seam allowance, or put a piece of tape on your machine to use as a guide. Practice makes
    perfect!
  4. Use coordinating thread.
  5. Use good quality materials. Poor quality fabric never looks as good as good quality fabric.
  6. Finish your seam allowances, and pick appropriate seam finishing techniques.
  7. Include details like topstitching and special trims to make your sewing look special.
  8. Slow down and enjoy the process. Sewing is just as much about the making process as it is about the
    finished result. It’s not a race!
  9. Hand baste when necessary. It will save you time and frustration and will save you from ripping
    stitches to re-sew.
  10. Don’t be afraid to use the seam ripper if something doesn’t work out exactly as you wanted. Even
    really skilled sewists rely on their seam ripper!
  11. Keep an eye on your stitch tension. Make sure the stitches are even and the thread doesn’t pucker.
  12. Make sure the fabric you’ve chosen is suitable for the pattern or style you’re making. Fabric
    suggestions on the back of the envelope can be helpful, and pay attention to similar styles and fabrics.
    This is a skill that you’ll learn with practice, too.
  13. Don’t feel like you need to make everything. Sew things that are at your skill level and that you feel
    comfortable making. And then wear them with things you’ve purchased so your wardrobe will look
    less homemade (in the bad sense).
  14. Study clothing in stores, in your closet, in vintage shops, and in museums to learn how it was
    constructed and to get ideas to improve your own sewing.
  15. Pay attention to current fashion so you stay up-to-date. Fashion changes over time, and your eye will
    change as well. Even classics need to be tweaked to look current.
